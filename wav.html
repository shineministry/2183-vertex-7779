How it works: for each block of samples, the tool tests a few fixed predictors (flat, linear, quadratic — same family FLAC uses) and keeps whichever leaves the smallest errors. Those errors are then packed with Rice coding, a form of entropy coding tuned for small numbers clustered near zero. Nothing about the actual sample values is altered — decoding replays the exact same math in reverse to rebuild the original waveform bit-for-bit. Output is a .wavc file; drop it back in here to restore the original .wav.
Runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.
